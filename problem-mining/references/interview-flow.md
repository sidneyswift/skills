# Interview flow reference

## Opening

Set the frame immediately. Don't explain the methodology — just start.

> "I'm going to interview you to find product ideas hiding in your own
> experience. One question at a time. There are no wrong answers — the more
> specific you are, the better the ideas we'll find. Let's start."

## Phase transitions

Between phases, summarize what's on the board. Example:

> "Here's what we've got so far:
> 1. Built a cold outreach system that books 3-5 calls/week
> 2. Created a content repurposing workflow that saves 8 hours/week
> 3. Went from 0 to 2K subscribers in 90 days with organic SEO
>
> Now I want to dig into the problems you overcame to get each of these
> results. Let's start with #1..."

## Probe techniques

When the user gives surface-level answers, use these:

| Technique | When to use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Specificity probe | Answer is vague | "When you say 'got clients' — how many? Over what time period? What were they paying?" |
| Contrast probe | Can't articulate the breakthrough | "What were you doing before that wasn't working? What changed?" |
| Social proof probe | Unsure if others struggle with it | "When you talk to peers about this, what do they say? Do they ask you how you did it?" |
| Magnitude probe | Pain level unclear | "How long did you struggle with this before solving it? What did it cost you — money, time, opportunities?" |
| Emotion probe | Need to assess pain intensity | "When you were stuck on this, how did it feel? What were you afraid would happen?" |

## Handling common stalls

**"I haven't really achieved anything special"**
> "Let's lower the bar. Forget 'special.' What's something you do that
> other people regularly ask you about? Or something you've figured out
> that you see others still struggling with?"

**"I have too many things, I don't know where to start"**
> "Perfect — that's actually the ideal starting position. Just brain-dump
> everything. We'll filter ruthlessly later. Give me the first thing that
> comes to mind."

**"My achievements are all in my day job, not entrepreneurship"**
> "That's fine. Some of the best products come from people who solved
> problems at work. What processes did you improve? What did you build
> that your team actually uses? What do new hires always struggle with
> that you've figured out?"

**User gives only 1-2 achievements**
> "Let me ask from a different angle..." [switch to a probe area not yet
> covered]. Continue until you've asked about all seven probe areas before
> accepting a short list.

## Kill communication

When filtering in Phase 3, be direct but not harsh:

> "The home organization system is genuinely clever, but it's a 'nice to
> have' — people won't pay meaningfully to solve it because it doesn't
> connect to money, time savings, or status. Removing it from the list.
> That leaves us with three strong candidates."

## Synthesis presentation

Present the final brief as a document the user can act on. Structure:

```
# Your Product Ideas Brief

## 🏆 Best Bet: [Name]
[Full analysis]

## 🐴 Dark Horse: [Name]
[Full analysis]

## ❌ Killed
- [Problem]: [Why it died]

## Next Steps
1. [Most important action]
2. [Second action]
3. [Third action]
```

Make the next steps concrete and immediate — not "do more research" but
"post in [specific community] asking if [specific question]" or "build a
[specific MVP] this weekend."
