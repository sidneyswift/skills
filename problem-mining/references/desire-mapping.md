# Desire mapping reference

## The four core desires

Every problem worth productizing traces back to at least one:

| Desire | What it looks like | Price sensitivity |
|---|---|---|
| **Making money** | "This will help me earn more" | Low — ROI math sells itself |
| **Saving money** | "This will reduce my costs" | Medium — savings must be concrete |
| **Saving time** | "This will give me hours back" | Medium — time must translate to value |
| **Status / attraction** | "This will change how people see me" | Variable — depends on audience |

## Mapping rules

### Strong signals (keep)
- "After solving this, I made $X more per month"
- "This saved me X hours every week that I now spend on Y"
- "People started seeing me as the expert in Z"
- "This cut my costs by X% and I could reinvest"

### Weak signals (probe deeper)
- "It made my life easier" → How? Quantify.
- "People were impressed" → Did that lead to opportunities, clients, offers?
- "It was satisfying" → Satisfying to whom? Did anyone else want the result?

### Kill signals (remove)
- "It was personally fulfilling but nobody else cares"
- "It's a hobby thing — nice to have"
- "People find it interesting but wouldn't pay for it"
- The only benefit is convenience with negligible time savings

## Multi-desire problems are gold

Problems that map to 2+ desires are exponentially more valuable:

- Making money + saving time = "I automated my client acquisition and now
  earn more while working less" → Premium pricing
- Status + making money = "I positioned myself as an authority and clients
  come to me at 3x rates" → High-ticket positioning
- Saving time + saving money = "I replaced a $500/mo tool and my manual
  process with one system" → Clear ROI story

## The "would they pay" test

For each problem, estimate willingness to pay:

| Level | Description | Product viability |
|---|---|---|
| **Aspirin** | Solves acute, immediate pain | High — people pay now |
| **Vitamin** | Improves something over time | Medium — harder to sell |
| **Candy** | Feels good, no real outcome | Low — impulse buy at best |

Only aspirin-level problems reliably support a product business.
Vitamin-level problems can work with strong proof and positioning.
Candy-level problems are content, not products — kill them.
