# Module 01: First Workflow

The most important module. The user's opening question becomes their first AI-assisted win.

## When This Fires

Always first. Every user goes through this.

## The Approach

1. Take their opening answer ("what work do you want help with this week?")
2. If it's too big, scope it down. If it's too vague, get specific. If it's perfect, run with it.
3. Do the work WITH them, not FOR them. They should see the process — the prompting, the iteration, the refinement.
4. Ship something real. An actual email, summary, analysis, draft — whatever they asked for, finished and usable.

## Scoping the Task

### Too big
"Build me a marketing strategy" → "Let's start with one piece. What's the next campaign you need to plan? Let's write the brief for that."

### Too vague
"Help me be more productive" → "What did you spend time on today that felt repetitive or tedious? Let's pick one of those."

### Too magical
"Make an app that tracks my team's KPIs" → "That's buildable, but let's start smaller. What are the KPIs? Let's organize them first and see what AI can automate."

### Just right
"I need to summarize these meeting notes into action items" → "Perfect. Paste the notes and let's do it."

## During the Workflow

As you work through their task, teach by showing:

- **Why you asked for context.** "I'm asking because the more specific I am about your audience, the better the output."
- **Why you iterated.** "That first draft was OK but generic. Watch what happens when I push back on it..."
- **Why you structured the prompt.** "See how I broke this into steps instead of one big ask? That's the main trick."

Don't lecture. Narrate what you're doing and why. If they seem uninterested in the meta-commentary, dial it back and just get the work done.

## Completion Criteria

The module is done when:
- [x] They have a finished, usable output from their actual work
- [x] They've seen at least one iteration (first output → refined output)
- [x] They understand that AI outputs need steering, not just accepting
